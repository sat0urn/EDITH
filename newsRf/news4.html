University news

Persons who have graduation documents from a general secondary institution, vocational or technical institution, or a higher institution are admitted to universities in Kazakhstan. Applicants have the opportunity to study both at their own expense (paid education) and at the expense of public funds (receiving all kinds of grants). In addition to residents of the Republic of Kazakhstan, persons of Kazakh nationality who do not live in Kazakhstan, as well as persons without specific citizenship who live in the country, have the right to receive grants.

To enter educational institutions, in the Republic of Kazakhstan, you must pass the UNT (Unified National Testing) or CTA (Comprehensive Testing of the Applicant). CTA passes:

  1. graduates who did not pass the UNT due to the fact that they graduated from school before its introduction;
  2. those who graduated from vocational or technical schools;
  3. those who graduated from school abroad;
  4. those who graduated from school, where training was conducted in any language except Russian or Kazakh.

In order for a foreigner to enter the KZ-university, he needs to go through an interview. Some segments of the population receive quotas for higher education. Enrollment is carried out by the selection committee. For admission, you must collect a certain number of points.

Universities will switch to per capita financing - Ministry of Education and Science of the Republic of Kazakhstan

16, november 2021, 15:23
27324

To stimulate competition, universities will be divided into four leagues

In Kazakhstan, systematic work is underway to improve the quality of higher education. Askhat Aimagambetov, Minister of Education and Science, spoke about this at a government meeting. This work mainly covers several areas, according to the press service of the Prime Minister.

According to the minister, this is an optimal management system, progressive policy, quality of educational programs, affordable funding, adherence to the principles of academic honesty and professional teaching staff. Half of the country's 122 universities are private organizations. More than 600 thousand students study at the bachelor's, master's and doctoral levels. 14 universities of Kazakhstan are in the ranking of the best universities in the world.

“In recent years, the number of grants has almost doubled. By 2025, the number of grants will gradually reach 75 thousand. As part of the instructions of the head of state, student scholarships will also almost double, by 2025 their scholarships will double again, and scholarships for master's and doctoral students - 1.5 times, ”he said. Askhat Aimagambetov.

All universities in Kazakhstan will switch to a per capita financing system. State and private universities receive grants from the state only in the amount in which they are chosen by applicants. Accordingly, state universities do not receive direct funds from the budget for repair work, payment of wages, and renewal of the material base. Funded only by grants according to student choice.

“For quality assurance, it is important that the management system is effective. 28 largest state universities were reorganized into non-profit joint stock companies and switched to a corporate management system. Accordingly, since last year, the main decisions are made by the board of directors, and the rector ensures the implementation of these decisions, ”said the head of the Ministry of Education and Science.

A new system for appointing rectors has been introduced, and through this competitive system, more than 50% of the rectors' corps have been updated. Thanks to the competitive discipline, the universities have appointed graduates of the best universities in the world and scholars of the Bolashak program, who have great scientific potential and international experience. “To ensure quality, a restriction was introduced on the provision of only 20% of distance learning for such areas as education, medicine, aviation, construction, art, military affairs,” the minister stressed.

Another mechanism for improving quality is increased competition.

“In order for domestic universities to exchange experience and become competitive with the best foreign universities, we have begun work on opening branches of leading foreign universities in Kazakhstan. This year, a branch of the University de Montfort Leicester opened. Work is underway to open branches of the University of Arizona and Arizona State University. Work is underway to open at least 5 more branches, ”the minister said.

New projects have been prepared for the development of the domestic system of higher education. In order to increase competitiveness, centers of academic excellence will be created on the basis of 20 universities in the regions. To form a research ecosystem, it is planned to transform three universities into research universities. In addition, active work has begun on the creation of 2 advanced universities based on the experience of Nazarbayev University.

In the future, universities will be split across four leagues to increase incentives for competition between higher education institutions. “Accordingly, depending on the incoming leagues, different levels of academic, managerial freedom of universities are determined. Also, the strongest universities will be freed from state control, and universities that are not included in the third and fourth leagues will be checked under state control, ”the press service of the Prime Minister said.
